Quote: "Lawmakers and regulators have increasingly expressed concern that TikTok and its parent company, ByteDance, may put sensitive user data, like location information, into the hands of the Chinese government."

The bill Biden signed gave TikTok’s Chinese parent, ByteDance, 270 days to sell TikTok or face a ban. The deadline is tomorrow, January 19, 2025. Originally, this meant that app stores and internet providers wouldn’t support TikTok anymore, effectively making it unusable.

But here’s the twist—just two days before the deadline, Biden announced he wouldn’t enforce the ban and decided to pass the responsibility to Trump instead. Why? Well, some might think it’s because Biden or the powers that be don’t want the 'Democrats' to look bad or be more hated than they already are. Either way, Biden’s administration is leaving it up to Trump, who might grant TikTok a 90-day extension to figure things out.

Meanwhile, TikTok is notifying its U.S. users about a possible shutdown, and content creators are scrambling to switch to platforms like Instagram and YouTube. The clock is still ticking, regardless.
